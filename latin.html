Lingua Latina

2024-12-25

Lingua latina was the language of the Romans and the ancestor of all Romance languages. It is a heavily inflected language.

There are three moods in Latin: the indicative, the imperative, and the subjunctive.

The indicative is most commonly used, and observes or recounts things that exist, have existed, or will very likely exist in the future.

Augustus est imperator. Augustus is the emperor.

The imperative is used to issue commands.

Sta! Stop!

The subjunctive is the mood of possibility: it may/could/should/would happen. However, the subjunctive also signifies different clauses:

  • temporal clause (cum ... subjunctive)
  • purpose clause (ut ... subjunctive)
    • indirect command (ABC verb ..., ut ... subjunctive)
