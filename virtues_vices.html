Virtues and Vices

2024-12-27

Virtue ethics generally says to do what a perfectly virtuous person would do in that situation. More generally, it says to avoid vices and choose virtues.

Aristotle's "Moral" Virtues:

Virtue Excess Deficiency
Courage Rashness Cowardice
Temperance Indulgense Insensibility
Liberality Prodigality Meanness
Magnificence Vulgarity Pettiness
Magnanimity Vanity Pusillanimity
Pride Empty Vanity Undue Humility
Patience Irascibility Lack of spirit
Truthfulness Boastfulness Understatement
Wittiness Buffoonery Boorishness
Friendliness Obsequiousness Cantankerousness
Modesty Shyness Shamelessness
Righteous indignation Envy Spitefulness

Aristotle's "Intellectual" Virtues:

Nous: Intelligence

Episteme: Science

Sophia: Theoretical wisdom

Gnome: Good sense

Synesis: Understanding

Phronesis: Practical wisdom

Techne: Art, craftmanship
